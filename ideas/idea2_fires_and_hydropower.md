# Idea 2 - Shifting Fire Regimes and Hydropower Stability in Washington

## Overall Concept

Examine how shifting wildfire regimes are affecting stability of hydropower basins in Washington state by using the USGS combined wildfire dataset plus watershed and dam location data. The team will derive decadal metrics of fire frequency, severity and size, and use machine learning models to identify basins where fire regimes have shifted toward larger or more frequent events in the past 20-30 years. Implications for water quality and sedimentation related stress.

### Resources and Data

-   USGS combined wildfire dataset

-   Hydropower dam locations in WA

-   National Hydrography Dataset for stream networks and catchments

-   Watershed boundaries (HUC-8 or HUC-12) covering dam locations

-   Land cover and slope data to identify which burned areas are most likely to cause sediment issues

### Implementation

Process data, derive metrics, train model and classify locations based on outputs.

### Problem Statement

Using historical wildfire and watershed data in WA state, quantify 30-year trends in fire activity for the upstream basins of hydropower projects within the state and build a simple model to classify risk levels. Use terrain and land-cover data to identify which basins are most likely to experience sediment and water quality impacts that could threaten hydropower stability.
